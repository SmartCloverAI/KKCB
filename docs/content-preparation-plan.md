# Content Preparation Plan

Date reviewed: `2026-04-22`

## Objective

Replace abstract, impersonal, and self-referential website copy with direct public-facing language that states:

- who Kids Kicking Cancer with Budo is locally
- what the organization does
- who it serves
- how the Budo method is used
- why volunteers, partners, and donors matter

## Tone Rules

- Use direct subjects: `Kids Kicking Cancer with Budo`, `capitolul din Bucuresti`, `organizatia`, `voluntarii`, `copiii`, `familiile`, `partenerii`.
- Prefer concrete verbs over abstract framing: `lucreaza`, `sprijina`, `formeaza`, `cauta`, `sustine`, `documenteaza`.
- State current reality before future ambition.
- Avoid sentences about the website itself unless the sentence is literally about navigation or legal publishing.
- Avoid vague institutional phrases such as `provide context`, `belongs to a wider continuity`, `transparent public engagement`, `support growth`, `with rigor`.
- Avoid product, planning, and rollout language such as `track`, `engine`, `next phase`, `conversion`, `what we are building`.
- Keep claims credible and bounded. Describe support, practice, documentation, and partnership goals without overstating outcomes.

## Page Goals

### Homepage

- State clearly that the Bucharest chapter brings Budo-based emotional regulation and bedside support to children in treatment.
- Name the three operating directions in concrete terms: hospital volunteering, research, fundraising.
- Explain support in terms of people and work, not website architecture.

### About

- Show the link between the Romanian chapter and the international history of Kids Kicking Cancer.
- Use the archive as proof of documented activity and continuity.
- Describe the Romanian chapter as an organization with a clear local mission, not as an abstract initiative.

### Method

- Explain the method in practical terms: breath, posture, voice, repetition, collaboration with medical staff.
- Show that the method is complementary to medical care.
- Keep the language human and specific.

### Research

- Speak in terms of pilot studies, university and hospital partnerships, and public results.
- Name the kinds of questions that can be studied.
- Present external references as useful models or resources, not as abstract “context.”

### Get Involved

- Name the groups that can help: volunteers, partners, donors.
- Explain what each group can contribute in plain language.
- Remove conversion or funnel language.

### Blog

- Frame the blog as a place for updates, examples, and explanations from the work.
- Avoid describing it as a strategic tool or growth engine.

### Archive / Evidence

- Explain what the photographs and references show in practical terms.
- Use the archive to support trust and continuity.
- Use external references to illustrate comparable work and credible public communication.

## Review Checklist

- Does each paragraph have a clear human or organizational subject?
- Would a parent, volunteer, clinician, or donor understand the point on first reading?
- Does the sentence describe activity, method, evidence, or need in concrete terms?
- Is the wording free of product, rollout, or strategy-memo language?
- Are Romanian and English versions aligned in meaning, not just in structure?
