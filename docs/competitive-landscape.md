# Competitive landscape

This note captures the official initiatives used as inspiration for the first KKCB Romania website direction.

## 1. MATIO / formerly Kids Kicking Cancer

Source:
- https://mymatio.org/
- https://mymatio.org/about-us/
- https://mymatio.org/resources/
- https://mymatio.org/get-involved/

Takeaways:
- Lead with the method, not with abstract charity language.
- Keep research resources visible instead of hiding them in PDFs or secondary menus.
- Make volunteering and fundraising feel like structured participation, not generic support.

## 2. Children with Cancer UK

Source:
- https://www.childrenwithcancer.org.uk/about-us/

Takeaways:
- Research, family support, and awareness can live in one clear information architecture.
- A serious nonprofit can still feel emotionally warm if the hierarchy is disciplined.
- Large editorial photography and strong typography build trust faster than dashboard-like layouts.

## 3. World Child Cancer

Source:
- https://worldchildcancer.org/mission-vision/

Takeaways:
- Use systems language for partnerships, public policy, and long-term change.
- Show that fundraising is part of a larger strategy, not the whole story.
- International collaboration should be visible in the narrative, not hidden in a footer.

## 4. Dăruiește Viață

Source:
- https://www.daruiesteviata.ro/en/proiecte/children-s-call-for-hope

Takeaways:
- Romanian audiences respond well to concrete project framing and visible transparency.
- Strong local credibility comes from specificity: where the work happens, what it changes, and what support enables.
- The site should make it easy to connect donations to real program layers.

## Implications for KKCB Romania

- The homepage should revolve around three tracks: bedside volunteering, research, fundraising.
- The blog should do strategic work: onboarding, evidence translation, and campaign storytelling.
- The archive matters. Historic photos and documentary material distinguish this chapter from a generic NGO launch.
